Treatment providers in substance abuse facilities have long recognized that interventions are needed to address the clinical needs of children living with a parent who abuses alcohol or other drugs, not only to treat their current difficulties, but also to help prevent the development of future emotional and behavioral problems that often emerge when these children enter adolescence and early adulthood. Family skills training programs have been evaluated rigorously by researchers and found to be effective in reducing a number of family, parent, and child risk factors for delinquency.
