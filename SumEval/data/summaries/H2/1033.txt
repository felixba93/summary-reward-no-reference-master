Involving parents in their own children's education at home is not enough.
Parent involvement is most effective when it is comprehensive, long-lasting, and well-planned.
Parents do not have to be well-educated to help.
Children tend to work harder at their studies when parents are involved.
Learn about the studying your child is expected to do.
Talk with your child about the importance of attending school regularly.
Teach your children to finish what they start.
Schools need to be mindful of these experiences when developing courses and training sessions for parents.
